# ADR-0002: Use Storyblok as Headless CMS for Content Management

## Status

Accepted

## Context

The application depends on a large volume of reference content (rules, conditions, actions, terminology) that must:

- be editable without rebuilding or redeploying the application;
- support RU/EN localization;
- be structured (typed fields, hierarchies, references);
- support preview/draft and published states.

The CMS choice directly impacts data modeling, integrations, caching, and editorial workflows.

Assumptions:

- Content in the MVP is primarily read-only for users.
- Preview/draft mode is required for editorial review.
- Vendor lock-in at the CMS level is acceptable for MVP/V1.

## Decision

- Use Storyblok as the headless CMS for content storage and management.
- Use published content in production and draft content in preview mode.
- Model content using structured components (content types) with explicit schemas and references.

## Alternatives Considered

1. **Contentful**
   - Why considered: popular platform with strong typing and ecosystem.
   - Why not chosen: cost/quotas and less convenient visual preview workflow for this project’s needs.
2. **Sanity**
   - Why considered: flexible schemas and strong developer experience.
   - Why not chosen: higher setup and maintenance overhead; MVP prioritizes fast, managed setup.
3. **Strapi (self-hosted)**
   - Why considered: full control over data and infrastructure.
   - Why not chosen: adds DevOps, hosting, security, and maintenance complexity inconsistent with MVP goals.
4. **Markdown/MDX in repository**
   - Why considered: simplicity and version control.
   - Why not chosen: poor editorial workflow, weaker structure, and content changes require redeploys.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Clear separation of content from code.
- Built-in localization, preview, and publishing workflows.
- Reduced need for custom admin tooling in MVP.

### Negative

- CMS vendor lock-in.
- Need to manage API tokens, quotas, and caching carefully.
- Requires disciplined content modeling to avoid schema drift.

### Risks

- Inconsistent content schemas over time.
- Latency or availability issues if CMS is queried directly at runtime.
- Mitigation: enforce content conventions, validate schemas, introduce a content access layer and caching.

## Notes (optional)

- CMS is a critical external dependency; graceful degradation and caching are required.
